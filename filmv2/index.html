The origins

of film

Another Form of

Expression

Film, like any art form, began as pure expression and experimentation. In this way, it was akin to a painting, no agenda to get across, no intended influence. In late 19th century Europe, in particular France, famous filmmakers and studios were being made. Film’s were silent and surreal, and people gathered in wonder and awe.

The beginning of an

influence

As film developed as a medium, sound was introduced, picture quality became better, the ways in which films were used changed. Films now didn’t have to be a motley of shadows, but could better portray an idea. The effects of this can be seen in the twentieth century.

The Consequences of

Expression

With film entirely recognized, and advanced, the obvious route was propaganda. In WWI the British were the most famous to use propaganda, and directors and the government worked on war films directed towards the US. In WWII, the use of calculated influence in film was only elevated, and the Nazis became the most infamous for their propaganda. Now, influence can be seen in the movie theater.
